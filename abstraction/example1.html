Abstraction Example #1

In Laurence’s first year of engineering, he super glued wires to a breadboard to make a circuit. He clearly had not taken electrical engineering yet. But how was he able to get the lights in his room to work every day? Is he still super gluing wires to a breadboard? How can he use this system, if he doesn’t have a foundational understanding of electric circuits yet? The answer is a light switch. Laurence doesn’t need to know how the circuit works – he just needs to be able to use it. To turn it off and on. The light switch hides the technical workings, and allows an end-user to utilise a system in a simplified way. This is the concept of abstraction.
