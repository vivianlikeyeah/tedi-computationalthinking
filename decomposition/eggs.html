Boiled Eggs


Ew...

Vivian does not like hard-boiled eggs. The kind and courteous staff at TEDI London have agreed to help Vivian collect all of the hard-boiled eggs in the world and hide them away in the storeroom.

Only spend a maximum of 15 minutes on this question – it may take you longer.


Solution
  • 1 hard-boiled egg == 1 raw egg
  • 12 raw eggs == 1 carton of eggs
  • 20 cartons of eggs == 1 fridge shelf
  • 10 fridge shelves == 1 fridge
  • 4 fridges in the storeroom
  • 4 * 10 * 20 * 12 = 9,600

INCORRECT Solution
Calculate the volume of a boiled egg. Calculate the volume of the room. Divide. Too few steps. Not breaking it down far enough. When you look at a large problem on its own, it can be quite overwhelming. Breaking down a problem into subproblems that are easy to solve, is the foundation of computational thinking. This is what we call decomposition. It reduces the complexity of the problem at hand, as well as giving you lots of opportunities to ‘sanity check’ your work. See Solution
